After reexamining, I now think isOnModulePath might still be relevant, I was just confused by two things: In ClasspathDirectory.equals, "module" is compared (see below) and there is some inconsistent naming: Many places where the value is passed for this still refer to automatic modules (the field was renamed from isAutoModule to isOnModulePath for bug 518282) - these naming issues should be fixed and the values checked. The relevant usage is in ClasspathJar.equals, which is used at least in two locations: 1) When the classpath locations are gathered in JavaSearchNameEnvironment.locationSet. A jar might appear both on the classpath and the modulepath. 2) When checking for classpath changes in JavaBuilder.hasClasspathChanged(). A change from classpath to modulepath might be relevant. For 2) I see a potential other issue: ClasspathDirectory sets "isOnModulePath" in the constructor, but in ClasspathDirectory.equals, "module" is compared, which can't work because it isn't persisted by State. It might be good enough to compare isOnModulePath there, too?

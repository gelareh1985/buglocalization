Annotation-based null type safety analysis is *potentially* a powerful technique for improving code quality. However, in practice there are *major* impediments to its easy and effective adoption: 1. the compiler's present inability to identify this particular case of 'non-null by declaration', leading to false positives; 2. the need for nullability annotations to be used assiduously throughout the entire source for a project; 3. the lack of *.eea files for the standard Java runtime library; 4. the lack of a tool for analysing non-annotated third party libraries to produce such *.eea files (from source or binary, ideally both). Item 4 offers an interim solution to Items 2 and 3. So in terms of priorities, solutions to items 1 and 4 would immediately and dramatically improve the ease of adoption and the accuracy of analyses.

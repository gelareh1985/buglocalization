Creation Time 2019-09-22T21:41:42Z
So the plan is this: 1. Add a new setting in Indentation section, under "Tab size:", called "Text block indentation:" with a combo containing following options: - Do not touch - Indent by one - Default indentation - Indent on column 2. The default will be "Default indentation" (so 2 tabs in built-in profiles). Most examples in JEP and popular articles use indentation on column, but that doesn't seem to fit the default profiles spirit (they never indent on column for wrapped code) and I don't see a reason to waste space this way. 3. Formatter will choose tabs or spaces according to "Tab policy" and will treat text blocks as wrapped lines (so will use spaces when "Use spaces to indent wrapped lines" is checked). 4. The formatter will consider tabs as 1 character wide for the purpose of counting accidental white space. This way the text block content after reformatting will be the same from the compiler's perspective, even though the indentation may look different in the text editor (when mixed tabs and spaces were used in the original source, unless bug 550561 comment 2 idea is implemented). When indenting on column with tabs, the formatter will assume their width according to the "Tab size" setting.
